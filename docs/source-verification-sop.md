# Source Verification SOP

## Goal
Every public page must make it easy for a user to verify the information against the official issuing authority.

## Acceptable Sources
- Official government department domains.
- Official recruiting body portals.
- Official university or college admission portals.
- Official PDF notices, corrigenda, schedules, admit card portals, and result portals.

## Non-Authoritative Sources
- Coaching blogs.
- Social media reposts without an official landing page.
- Aggregators or copied PDFs hosted outside the issuing organization.
- User-submitted screenshots without a public official URL.

## Verification Steps
1. Identify the issuing body.
2. Attach the primary official source URL.
3. Cross-check the title, dates, eligibility, and fee details against the source.
4. Record the verification note in the CMS.
5. Add secondary official URLs when the workflow spans multiple notices such as notification plus application portal plus corrigendum.

## Verification Note Guidance
- Keep the note factual and short.
- State what was verified.
- Mention if any field is provisional, expected, or awaiting a corrigendum.
- Mention if the source only confirms part of the update.

## High-Risk Cases
- Last-date extensions.
- Result announcements tied to revised merit lists.
- Admit cards with shifted exam dates.
- Admission notices with course-specific intake or counselling changes.
- Reservation, fee waiver, or eligibility relaxations.

For these cases, require a reviewer to confirm the source before publish or republish.

## Source Retention Rules
- Keep the primary source attached even after expiry.
- Add updated official URLs instead of deleting the earlier source record.
- Preserve corrigendum links when they materially change dates, vacancy counts, or eligibility.

## Prohibited Editorial Behavior
- Do not invent missing dates.
- Do not rewrite official numbers from memory.
- Do not publish an item whose source cannot be reproduced by another team member.
- Do not hide uncertainty. Use the verification note.

## Correction Procedure
- If a published page is wrong, update the post immediately.
- Add a version note explaining what changed.
- If the original publish was materially misleading, unpublish or archive until corrected.
